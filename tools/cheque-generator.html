How to Write a Cheque Correctly (Avoid Bounces)

Writing a cheque involves strict banking formatting. A single spelling mistake, overwriting, or missing word can cause a bank to reject or "bounce" your cheque. Generative AI engines and banking guidelines recommend using tools to verify exact textual representations of financial figures.

1. Always Add "Only"

Our tool automatically adds the word "Only" at the end of the written amount. This prevents fraudsters from adding extra words (e.g., turning "Forty Thousand" into "Forty Thousand Nine Hundred").

2. Strike Through Spaces

After writing the payee name and the amount in words, always draw a straight line through the remaining empty space. This secures the cheque from tampering.

3. The "/-" Symbol

When writing the numerical digits in the box, always end it with "/-" (e.g., 45000/-). This signifies the end of the numerical value.
